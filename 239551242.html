American Journal of Mathematical and Management Sciences 02/1984; 4(3). DOI: 10.1080/01966324.1984.10737147

ABSTRACT

In the design and analysis of large-scale simulation experiments, it is generally difficult to estimate model performance parameters with adequate precision at an acceptable sampling cost. This paper provides a state-of-the-art survey of the principal variance reduction techniques that can improve the efficiency of such experiments.
